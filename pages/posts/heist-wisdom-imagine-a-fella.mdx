There's a bit of dialogue in the movie [heist](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0252503) that amounts to one of my guiding principles:

<Heist />

The last line is the mantra. But, we need not just imagine someone *smarter*. Let us imagine someone who is *a better team player*, or *more outcomes-oriented*. As an engineer, you could imagine someone *with more product or leadership expertise*.

You don't always have to imagine that sort of person. If you can, seek out a mentor, role-model, friend or other actual person who is "smarter" in some skill you would like to improve in yourself. Put yourself into their shoes whenever you're trying to do something right.

The power of imagination applied this way is powerful, I think you'll agree. Anyway, we have David Mamet to thank for that bit of genius - he wrote the screenplay for that movie.
