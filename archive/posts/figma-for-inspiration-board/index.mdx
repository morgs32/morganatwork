Using Figma for a mood board

How do you save or capture good web designs? Skitch? A directory of screenshots on your computer? Bookmarks? I've tried most of them. I've tried using Dropbox's Paper. I've tried Notion. And I'm settled on Figma as being the best choice. It's a conclusion that seems obvious now - put your favorite designs in your favorite design tool. But it wasn't at first.

My hesitation with using Figma in the first place probably had to do with some conveniences I couldn't find. I wanted to be able to save a website or screenshot via a chrome extension or keyboard shortcut. But really - how much time am I trying to save? After all, that screenshot deserves a little more intention that a keyboard shortcut I've committed to muscle memory. It warrants at least a sentence or two about "why it caught my eye" or "what specific features or UI components are relevant to the project I have in mind".  

Then, I thought I wanted a repository for these screenshots that would be tabulated and practically quantitative. But again, in hindsight... why? So I could create an end of year pie chart of how many screenshots I had taken by industry or some other category? That, sir (or madame), is called scope creep.

Finally, I wanted to be able to circle, draw and annotate these screenshots. So I could hone in on the real elements of inspiration. That left me with even fewer options.

After all the rest, I tried Figma in some sense of defeat - like I had gone speed dating for a new software solution to my problem and didn't match with anyone. But using Figma is so easy, and I love having everything on one artboard. It's one less tool to deal with, does everything I need it to and plenty that I don't (such as being able to share it and take comments).
